Using Machine Learning to Connect Hydrodynamical Simulations of Star Collisions with Stellary Dynamics Simulations in Dense Galactic Centers



Abstract

Dense clusters of stars surround the supermassive black hole (SMBH) in the galactic nuclei. Interactions between the stars in this cluster can alter to orbits of stars, as well as their properties such as mass, size, and age. Due to these interactions, stars can also experience Tidal Disruption Events (TDEs) where they approach too closely to the SMBH and are torn apart. They can also experience Mutual Destruction Events (MDEs) where they collide directly with another star in the cluster, causing the complete disruption of both stars. Predicting the outcome of these collisions from initial conditions can be difficult and computationally expensive, so we can emulate high precision simulations of these collisions using machine learning (ML). In this paper, we present the first model of a nuclear star cluster that uses ML to bridge large sets of smoothed particle hydrodynamic (SPH) simulations of star collisions with stellar dynamic simulations. Using our ML model to predict the outcome of these collisions, we can more accurately predict how these stellar clusters evolve through their mass and orbits. We find that our ML model's predictions align with previous studies and MDEs are relatively common within the 0.01 pc region of the cluster. Future work will improve the nuances of this model by including a more realistic mass spectrum to the cluster and gravitational focusing.
